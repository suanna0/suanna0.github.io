Keychain

ZBrush, Maya, DaVinci Resolve, 2024.

Personal project.

Process:

I drew inspiration from collectible toys such as Smiskis, Miffy, and Astro Boy. To avoid over-complicating the form, I looked for ways to communicate a human form with as few shapes as possible. I studied the proportions of various figurines and I noticed that a big head, small torso, and large torso, tended to be successful.

Initial Sketches. As I made more iterations, my form became simpler./p>

Here is my final sketch before modeling on ZBrush.

Retopologizing the sculpture.

Rigging–The goal of rigging was to create some muscle systems along the form as if the mask had a life of its own. However, this did not end up being necessary in the final render since the dancer moved a lot and the viewer would not be able to notice subtle changes in the mask's shape.

Finished retop and rig capabilities.

Rendering–During filming, we mapped the nodes onto the dancer's face that corresponded with the nodes in the mask. Instead of mapping one-to-one in every frame in the video, I decided to render every other frame to emphasize the artificial quality of the mask.

Mapping joints onto video.

Rendering mask onto video (before final render).
