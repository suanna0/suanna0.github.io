3464 19th Street

Oil on two wooden panels (diptych), 10 x 10 inches each, 2025.

Timeline: 3 weeks

This piece is a response to the work of Matias Sauter Morea's Pegamachos.

"Trauma porn" is a problem in the contemporary art world for marginalized communities. In a market that is rooted in elitism, where work is often interpreted to be collectible items, “conquered” by the ruling class, creating artwork based on the intricacies of marginalized identities can quickly feel exploitative.

However, the artwork, Pegamachos by Matias Sauter Morea, suggests a solution. Morea uses AI-generated imagery to represent lost history in Costa-Rican Queer history, while protecting the privacy of queer folk. I saw this as a lead to exploration, not a end-all solution–the final images did not feel authentic to me. The images felt staged and performative, the poses were hyper-perfect, in service for the camera's gaze.

This work seeks to educate people on the queer history of SF, creating imagery that feels authentic, without “exploiting” people's privacy.
