About

Suanna Zhong is a fourth-year student in the Bachelor of Computer Science and Arts (BCSA) program at Carnegie Mellon University, studying Computer Science and Art through the School of Computer Science and the College of Fine Arts. She’s interested in how technical and artistic practices inform each other—often through building websites, color-grading, or filmmaking.

Being in this unique position between disciplines—and having experience teaching Computer Science—she hopes that her work can help technical research breakthroughs become more legible to broader audiences. Hence, she enjoys being in conversation with artists, engineers, and anyone who feels like they fall somewhere in between.

Her clients span a wide range of industries, from tech to contemporary art and global music, including Capitol Records, Common Sense Media, DAY6, Dehancer, Jeremy Zucker, JYP Entertainment, Kali Uchis, Kid Cudi, Marcus Rahauser, Meta/Facebook, Pinterest, Republic Records, Stray Kids, Virgin Music Group, and VSCO.

When she’s not working, you can probably find her making art, listening to jazz, cafe hopping, or knitting winter clothes.
