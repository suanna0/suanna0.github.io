Suanna Zhong

I am a junior at Carnegie Mellon studying Computer Science and Fine Art with a minor in Animation and Special Effects. The computer is not my enemy. Do not tell me to paint French gardens when so much of my time is spent gazing at the computer.

I come from a family of engineers. Hence, a lot of my work tells stories of my experiences growing up queer in an Asian household, my relationship to CS, and what my identity means in relation to pre-existing societal structures. I enjoy making work about my family as I find it interesting to compare and contrast how our struggles have changed over a generation, and what events have shaped our values. I also explore how queerness is more than someone's identity, it can be an ideology for an individual to live their life by.
